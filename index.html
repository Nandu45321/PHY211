Fundamental Laws of Thermodynamics

üî• First Law of Thermodynamics

$$dU = \delta Q - \delta W$$ $$\Delta U = Q - W$$

Where: U = Internal Energy, Q = Heat added to system, W = Work done by system

‚ô®Ô∏è Second Law of Thermodynamics

$$dS \geq \frac{\delta Q}{T}$$ $$\Delta S_{universe} \geq 0$$

Clausius Statement: Heat cannot flow spontaneously from cold to hot.

Kelvin-Planck Statement: No heat engine can convert all heat into work.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Third Law of Thermodynamics

$$\lim_{T \to 0} S = 0$$

Entropy of a perfect crystal at absolute zero is zero.

‚ö° Fundamental Thermodynamic Relations

$$dU = TdS - PdV + \mu dN$$ $$H = U + PV$$ $$F = U - TS$$ $$G = H - TS = U + PV - TS$$

Where: H = Enthalpy, F = Helmholtz Free Energy, G = Gibbs Free Energy

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Work Formulas

$$W = \int P \, dV$$ $$W_{reversible} = nRT \ln\left(\frac{V_f}{V_i}\right)$$ (Isothermal Process) $$W = \frac{nR(T_f - T_i)}{1-\gamma}$$ (Adiabatic Process)

Thermodynamic Cycles & Heat Engines

üöÇ Carnot Cycle

$$\eta_{Carnot} = 1 - \frac{T_C}{T_H} = \frac{T_H - T_C}{T_H}$$ $$\frac{Q_H}{T_H} = \frac{Q_C}{T_C}$$

Steps: Isothermal expansion ‚Üí Adiabatic expansion ‚Üí Isothermal compression ‚Üí Adiabatic compression

‚öôÔ∏è Otto Cycle

$$\eta_{Otto} = 1 - \frac{1}{r^{\gamma-1}}$$

Where: r = compression ratio = \(V_{max}/V_{min}\)

Steps: Adiabatic compression ‚Üí Isochoric heating ‚Üí Adiabatic expansion ‚Üí Isochoric cooling

üîß Diesel Cycle

$$\eta_{Diesel} = 1 - \frac{1}{\gamma} \cdot \frac{r_c^{\gamma} - 1}{r^{\gamma-1}(r_c - 1)}$$

Where: r = compression ratio, \(r_c\) = cutoff ratio

‚ùÑÔ∏è Refrigerators and Heat Pumps

$$COP_{ref} = \frac{Q_C}{W} = \frac{Q_C}{Q_H - Q_C} = \frac{T_C}{T_H - T_C}$$ (Carnot Refrigerator) $$COP_{HP} = \frac{Q_H}{W} = \frac{Q_H}{Q_H - Q_C} = \frac{T_H}{T_H - T_C}$$ (Carnot Heat Pump)

‚ö†Ô∏è Efficiency Relations

$$\eta = \frac{W_{net}}{Q_{in}} = 1 - \frac{Q_{out}}{Q_{in}}$$ $$\eta_{any} \leq \eta_{Carnot}$$

Entropy & Free Energy

üåÄ Entropy Calculations

$$dS = \frac{\delta Q_{rev}}{T}$$ $$S = k_B \ln \Omega$$ (Boltzmann's Formula) $$\Delta S = nC_v \ln\left(\frac{T_f}{T_i}\right) + nR\ln\left(\frac{V_f}{V_i}\right)$$ (Ideal Gas)

üîÑ Entropy Change for Common Processes

Isothermal: \(\Delta S = nR\ln\left(\frac{V_f}{V_i}\right) = \frac{Q}{T}\)
Isobaric: \(\Delta S = nC_p\ln\left(\frac{T_f}{T_i}\right)\)
Isochoric: \(\Delta S = nC_v\ln\left(\frac{T_f}{T_i}\right)\)
Adiabatic: \(\Delta S = 0\) (reversible)
Phase Change: \(\Delta S = \frac{L}{T}\)

‚ö° Maxwell Relations

$$\left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial V}\right)_S = -\left(\frac{\partial P}{\partial S}\right)_V$$ $$\left(\frac{\partial T}{\partial P}\right)_S = \left(\frac{\partial V}{\partial S}\right)_P$$ $$\left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial V}\right)_T = \left(\frac{\partial P}{\partial T}\right)_V$$ $$\left(\frac{\partial S}{\partial P}\right)_T = -\left(\frac{\partial V}{\partial T}\right)_P$$

üéØ Free Energy Criteria

Helmholtz Free Energy (F): Minimum at equilibrium (constant T, V)

$$dF = -SdT - PdV$$ $$\Delta F = \Delta U - T\Delta S$$

Gibbs Free Energy (G): Minimum at equilibrium (constant T, P)

$$dG = -SdT + VdP$$ $$\Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S$$

Kinetic Theory of Gases

üé± Ideal Gas Relations

$$PV = nRT = Nk_BT$$ $$PV^\gamma = \text{constant}$$ (Adiabatic) $$\gamma = \frac{C_p}{C_v}$$

Where: \(v_{rms}\) = root mean square speed, \(\bar{v}\) = average speed, \(v_{mp}\) = most probable speed

üí° Exam Tips & Common Pitfalls

  • Sign Conventions: W is positive when work is done BY the system
  • Units: Always check that R has correct units for your calculation
  • Reversible Process: Maximum work is obtained in reversible processes
  • Entropy: Always increases for isolated systems (2nd Law)
  • Carnot Efficiency: Maximum possible efficiency between two temperatures
  • State Functions: U, H, S, F, G are path-independent
  • Process Functions: Q and W are path-dependent
  • Adiabatic: No heat transfer, but temperature can change
  • Isothermal: ŒîU = 0 for ideal gas (U depends only on T)
